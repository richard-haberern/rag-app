Answers grounded in your own documents.

Groundwork ingests your text, chunks and embeds it locally, retrieves the most relevant passages with vector search, and has an LLM answer from them — so every answer is tied to your source material, not the model's training data.

Under the hood

Multi-tenant from the ground up. Every request runs under a real session — a one-click anonymous session or a username/password account (argon2, opaque database-backed session tokens). Tenants are isolated by Postgres row-level security, and every credential and session write goes through SECURITY DEFINER functions, so the least-privilege application role never touches the auth tables directly.

One datastore, atomic by design. Documents, chunks and vectors (pgvector) live in a single Postgres database, so storing or deleting a document is one transaction — it fully happens or fully rolls back, with no orphaned chunks or vectors. Embeddings are computed locally with sentence-transformers; generation runs against Gemini over raw HTTP, with no vendor SDK in the path. A layered store/service architecture keeps persistence and business logic apart, and ORM objects never escape the store layer.
